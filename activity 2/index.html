Welcome to Amsterdam!

A vibrant city known for its picturesque canals, rich history, world-class museums, and lively culture

Explore The City

Amsterdam, the capital of the Netherlands, is a vibrant and picturesque city known for its rich history, stunning architecture, and unique canals. The city's iconic 17th-century canal ring, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is lined with charming gabled houses, museums, and bustling cafes. Amsterdam is often referred to as the "Venice of the North" due to its extensive canal system, which offers a picturesque setting for both residents and visitors.

Rijksmuseum

The National Museum

The Rijksmuseum is a renowned Dutch national museum located in Amsterdam, Netherlands. It is famous for its extensive collection of art and historical artifacts, including masterpieces from the Dutch Golden Age by artists like Rembrandt, Vermeer, and Frans Hals. The museum is situated at Museumplein, a cultural hub in the city, and is one of the most visited museums in the world.

Van Gogh Museum

A journey through Van Gogh's vibrant world

The Van Gogh Museum in Amsterdam’s Museumplein holds the world’s largest collection of Vincent van Gogh’s art, including iconic works like Sunflowers and The Bedroom. Offering insights into his life and letters, the museum reveals his evolving style and impact on modern art.

Anne Frank's House

A writer's house and biographical museum

The Anne Frank House, located on Prinsengracht in Amsterdam, is a historic museum in the secret annex where Anne Frank wrote her famous diary during WWII, offering a moving glimpse into her life and legacy.

The Floating Flower Market (Bloemenmarkt)

While not everything here is free, simply walking through the Bloemenmarkt, the world’s only floating flower market, is an enjoyable and sensory experience. You’ll find vibrant tulip displays and other flowers in the stalls floating on the Singel canal.

Keukenhof Garden

Keukenhof is one of the most famous flower gardens in the world, located in Lisse, Netherlands. It’s known for its tulips and other beautiful flowers in bloom.

Hortus Botanicus

Founded in 1638, Hortus Botanicus is one of the oldest botanical gardens in the world. With greenhouses full of exotic plants, palm trees, and succulents, this hidden oasis is a serene escape from the bustling city.

×

Herengracht Canal

Herengracht is the most prestigious of Amsterdam’s three major canals, often referred to as the "gentleman's canal." Walking along this canal gives you a glimpse into Amsterdam’s Golden Age, with beautiful 17th-century homes lining the water.

Windmills

No visit to the Netherlands is complete without a trip to see a traditional Dutch windmill. While Amsterdam itself has a few windmills, Molen Van Sloten in the west of the city is a favourite for its open access and history. climb the windmill for panoramic views of the Dutch countryside.

Oude Kerk

Amsterdam’s oldest building, the Oude Kerk (Old Church), is a stunning piece of mediaeval architecture located in the heart of the Red Light District. Its historic facade juxtaposed against the lively district makes for an intriguing photo op. Head inside to explore its Gothic interiors and admire the beautiful stained-glass windows.
